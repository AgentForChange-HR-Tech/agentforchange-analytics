Where people analytics stands in 2026
From Reporting to Prediction to Action
Evolution Stage

People analytics has moved through three distinct phases: (1) Descriptive — what happened (headcount, turnover, time-to-hire). (2) Predictive — what will happen (flight risk models, talent supply forecasts). (3) Prescriptive/Agentic — what to do about it. Most organizations are somewhere between phases 1 and 2. The leading edge is phase 3: AI agents that not only surface workforce insights but take action — flagging retention risks, triggering development interventions, rebalancing headcount plans in real time.

Visier launched its Analytic AI Agent Platform — enabling HR and IT teams to create and deploy AI agents that analyze and act on people and work data. Moving beyond traditional people analytics toward agentic workforce intelligence — not just surfacing insights but integrating them into the tools and processes where decisions are made.

Data Governance as the Foundation
Critical Prerequisite

The most common failure mode in people analytics is building sophisticated models on top of unreliable data. Clean people data and governance standards must precede AI deployment — not follow it. This means unified employee identifiers across HRIS, ATS, LMS, and payroll; agreed definitions for key metrics (what counts as a voluntary termination?); and data stewardship ownership within HR. Without this foundation, every analytics output is suspect and every AI agent learns from corrupted inputs.

Skills Intelligence as the New Currency
Strategic Priority

The shift to skills-based organizations requires a skills intelligence infrastructure that doesn't yet exist in most companies. Skills taxonomies must connect learning, performance, and career development systems. Internal skill matching must be measured and prioritized before external recruitment. Workforce planning models must incorporate skill acquisition velocity — not just headcount. Platforms like Gloat, Eightfold, Lightcast, and Draup are building the market intelligence layer that sits on top of internal skills data.

Analytics maturity model — where is your org?
Level 1
Operational Reporting
Headcount, turnover rates, time-to-hire from a single system. Mostly manual. Reactive. Most HR teams are here.
Level 2
Cross-System Analytics
Data integrated across HRIS, ATS, LMS. Dashboards and visualizations. Insights are available but not yet predictive.
Level 3
Predictive Analytics
Flight risk models, talent supply forecasting, diversity pipeline analysis. Models inform decisions but humans still act.
Level 4
Prescriptive Intelligence
Analytics surfaces recommendations, not just insights. Systems suggest next best actions. Managers receive alerts before problems escalate.
Level 5
Agentic Workforce Intelligence
AI agents act on analytics insights autonomously — flagging risks, triggering workflows, rebalancing plans. Visier MCP and similar tools represent this frontier.

High-value analytics use cases — 2026
Retention Risk Prediction

Models that identify employees at risk of leaving 3–6 months before they resign. Inputs: tenure, performance trajectory, compensation vs. market, manager quality, engagement signals, absence patterns. High ROI — replacing a technical employee costs 50–200% of annual salary.

Workforce Supply & Demand Forecasting

Predictive models connecting business growth plans to hiring needs, skills gaps, and internal mobility opportunities. Critical for semiconductor companies planning 18-month chip development cycles — talent constraints are known risks, not surprises.

Diversity Pipeline Analytics

Tracking representation at every stage of the talent funnel — from sourcing to offer acceptance to promotion to leadership. Identifies where and when diverse candidates are lost. HiredScore (Workday) applies AI to this problem at the screening stage.

Manager Effectiveness Scoring

Correlating manager behaviors with team outcomes — engagement, retention, promotion rates, performance. Surfaces which managers are multiplying talent and which are suppressing it. Enables targeted development investments and succession planning.

Pay Equity Analysis

Statistical modeling that identifies unexplained pay gaps across gender, race, and other protected characteristics after controlling for legitimate factors. Increasingly required by state transparency laws. Bersin's HR 2030 vision includes AI agents that continuously monitor compensation equity without annual audit cycles.
